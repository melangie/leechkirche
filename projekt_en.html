About the project

The aim of this project is to digitally capture the Leechkirche in Graz using the latest 3D modelling techniques and make it accessible in an interactive form. The Leechkirche, an important cultural site with a rich history and architecture, should be able to be used both for educational purposes and to promote cultural heritage through the creation of a comprehensive digital archive.

The Leechkirche, centrally located in Graz, has its origins in the 14th century and is an impressive example of different architectural styles, ranging from Gothic to modern times. Originally built as part of a monastery, it has played an important role in the local community over the centuries and is adorned with ornate frescoes and carvings that emphasise its cultural and religious significance.

Aims of the project

  • Digital recording: By using 3D modelling techniques, the aim is to achieve a precise digital record of the Leech Church. This enables a detailed reconstruction of its architecture and artistic elements.

  • Interactive experience: The project aims to create an interactive experience that allows users to explore the Leech Church digitally. This includes virtual tours where visitors can view the church from different angles.

  • Educational purposes: The digital archive will be used for educational purposes, especially for schools and universities. It will enable students and researchers to study the architecture, history and cultural significance of the Leech Church.

  • Cultural heritage: The digital presentation of the Leech Church will help to preserve its cultural heritage and make it accessible to the general public. This is of particular importance as the church is an important part of the history of Styria.

Special significance of the presentation of medicinal herbs

A remarkable feature of the Leech Church is the depiction of medicinal herbs in its frescoes and carvings. These depictions refer to the long tradition of herbal medicine in the region. The herbs were not only valued for their healing properties, but were also symbolically integrated into the art and architecture of the church. This illustrates the close connection between religion and medicine in medieval society.

The project for the digital preservation and presentation of the Leechkirche in Graz represents a significant step towards the preservation and promotion of this cultural heritage. By digitally recording and creating an interactive experience, the Leechkirche is made accessible to a wide audience and contributes to the education and appreciation of its history and architecture. Of particular note is the display of medicinal herbs, which offers insights into medieval culture and its connection to medicine.
